Logo Design Competition

CC Global Summit 2015 is held in Seoul, South Korea, in October 2015, to host people who believe in the values of sharing and openness and create positive change in the world.

In anticipation of the Creative Commons Global Summit 2015, Creative Commons Korea, the host of the event, is pleased to launch a Logo Design Competition.

From established professional designers to students and amateurs, anyone with creative talent are welcome to join!

 

Update: We're now pleased to announce that the deadline for the Logo Design Competition has been extended to July 7! Please check the updated schedule below.

Submit

  • How to enter via competition website
  • Schedule June 8, 2015 to July 7, 2015 (UTC)
  • Selection
    • Entries will be judged jointly by the public and the CC Selection Committee
    • 1st Round: Public voting (July 8 - July 15)
    • 2nd Round: Judging by CC Selection Committee (July 16 - July 21)
    • Results: July 23, 2015
  • Prize cash prize of $500 (The winner is solely responsible for all applicable taxes related to the acceptance of the prize, which will differ depending on the jurisdiction where they reside) and recognition at the Global Summit.)

Submission guidelines

Creative Commons has a globally recognized visual identity, which has been recently celebrated by both MOMA and the Noun Project. This is your opportunity to add your creativity and skill to this identity, by helping us produce a unique branding for the Global Summit. The winning logo will be used online, in print, and on merchandise.

Requirements

  • As with prior logo competitions, winners will be required to assign all rights in the winning design to Creative Commons in consideration of the cash prize.
  • Entries should be provided in PNG file formats (the winner will be asked to provide the design in SVG formats)
  • If you are using works of others in your design, make sure they are free from copyright restrictions. You must identify and provide attribution to the creator of any such works and indicate that you have the right to use their works in your original creation.
